# Super - Replication Guide

> How to build a focused product inspired by Super

## Overview

Creators and small teams like writing in Notion but need a public website with custom domains, SEO, and polished design. Super turns Notion into a site backend. The public metric snapshot shows $96,486 MRR across 5,137 customers. The lesson is not to copy the brand, but to study the narrow workflow, distribution channel, and pricing model.

## Market Opportunity

**Niche:** No-code website publishing built on top of Notion.

**Why it is underserved:** Broad tools tend to ignore the exact workflow, vocabulary, and integrations that a narrow user group needs. A small product can win by being easier to adopt than a generic platform.

**Why now:** Buyers are comfortable subscribing to specialized tools when the product saves time, removes manual work, or plugs into a system they already use.

## MVP Build Guide

### Core Features

1. **Connect to Notion pages and render them as public routes.**
2. **Add custom domains, caching, SEO fields, themes, and redirects.**
3. **Build templates and an onboarding flow that converts an existing Notion page in minutes.**

### Build Time and Cost

- **Build time:** 4-6 weeks
- **Starting cost:** $50-150/mo

## Pricing Strategy

Subscription per website. Use a free preview tier, then $12-29/site/mo for custom domains and advanced features.

## Customer Acquisition

1. Rank for Notion website and Notion templates keywords.
2. Build a template gallery with creator examples.
3. Partner with Notion consultants and template sellers.

## Unit Economics

- Start with low-touch onboarding if the price is below $50/mo.
- Use concierge onboarding if customers pay more than $100/mo.
- Watch support time per account; niche products can become service businesses if workflows are unclear.

## Common Pitfalls

1. Notion API and rendering edge cases can create support load.
2. Customers expect strong SEO and speed even though content starts in Notion.
3. Template quality matters more than raw feature count.

## Launch Checklist

### Pre-Launch
- [ ] Interview 10 target users and collect exact workflow language.
- [ ] Build the smallest workflow that produces a paid result.
- [ ] Add billing, onboarding, support email, and basic analytics.

### Launch
- [ ] Invite the first 20 prospects manually.
- [ ] Publish a comparison or teardown post for the existing manual process.
- [ ] Offer high-touch setup for the first paying customers.

### Post-Launch
- [ ] Turn repeated support questions into onboarding screens.
- [ ] Track activation, churn reasons, and top integrations.
- [ ] Add one acquisition channel at a time.
