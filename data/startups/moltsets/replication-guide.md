# MoltSets - Replication Guide

> How to build a focused product inspired by MoltSets

## Overview

Very niche operators often need workflow software that broad SaaS tools ignore. MoltSets shows how a focused product can still support paid subscriptions with a narrow customer base. The public metric snapshot shows $1,935 MRR across 15 customers. The lesson is not to copy the brand, but to study the narrow workflow, distribution channel, and pricing model.

**Key Metrics:**
- MRR: $1,935
- Customers: 15
- Source: Baremetrics Open Startups
- Category: SaaS
- Team: Small team

## Market Opportunity

**Niche:** Vertical micro-SaaS for specialized workflows.

**Why it is underserved:** Broad tools tend to ignore the exact workflow, vocabulary, and integrations that a narrow user group needs. A small product can win by being easier to adopt than a generic platform.

**Why now:** Buyers are comfortable subscribing to specialized tools when the product saves time, removes manual work, or plugs into a system they already use.

## MVP Build Guide

### Core Features

1. **Interview a narrow user group and map the recurring workflow they currently run in spreadsheets.**
2. **Build the smallest CRUD workflow with saved templates, exports, and reminders.**
3. **Add billing, onboarding examples, and support loops for the first 10 paying customers.**

### Build Time and Cost

- **Build time:** 3-4 weeks
- **Starting cost:** $30-100/mo

## Pricing Strategy

Simple monthly subscription. Start at $19-49/mo and use high-touch onboarding to prove value.

## Customer Acquisition

1. Direct outreach into the niche community.
2. Create templates that mirror the spreadsheet people already use.
3. Ask early customers for referrals inside the vertical.

## Unit Economics

- Start with low-touch onboarding if the price is below $50/mo.
- Use concierge onboarding if customers pay more than $100/mo.
- Watch support time per account; niche products can become service businesses if workflows are unclear.

## Common Pitfalls

1. Niche does not mean no competition; spreadsheets are the main competitor.
2. Feature requests can scatter the product if every customer is different.
3. Small customer count means churn is very visible.

## Launch Checklist

### Pre-Launch
- [ ] Interview 10 target users and collect exact workflow language.
- [ ] Build the smallest workflow that produces a paid result.
- [ ] Add billing, onboarding, support email, and basic analytics.

### Launch
- [ ] Invite the first 20 prospects manually.
- [ ] Publish a comparison or teardown post for the existing manual process.
- [ ] Offer high-touch setup for the first paying customers.

### Post-Launch
- [ ] Turn repeated support questions into onboarding screens.
- [ ] Track activation, churn reasons, and top integrations.
- [ ] Add one acquisition channel at a time.
