# Friendly - Replication Guide

> How to build a focused product inspired by Friendly

## Overview

Teams want analytics and marketing automation without heavy surveillance, cookie banners, or complex enterprise tooling. Friendly packages privacy-first marketing tools as a managed service. The public metric snapshot shows CHF6,440 MRR across 60 customers. The lesson is not to copy the brand, but to study the narrow workflow, distribution channel, and pricing model.

**Key Metrics:**
- MRR: CHF6,440
- Customers: 60
- Source: Baremetrics Open Startups
- Category: Marketing
- Team: Small team

## Market Opportunity

**Niche:** Privacy-first marketing operations and hosted open source alternatives.

**Why it is underserved:** Broad tools tend to ignore the exact workflow, vocabulary, and integrations that a narrow user group needs. A small product can win by being easier to adopt than a generic platform.

**Why now:** Buyers are comfortable subscribing to specialized tools when the product saves time, removes manual work, or plugs into a system they already use.

## MVP Build Guide

### Core Features

1. **Choose one privacy-first workflow: analytics, email automation, CRM, or consent-friendly reporting.**
2. **Host and manage the underlying open source tool with backups, updates, and support.**
3. **Package onboarding, templates, and compliance documentation as the paid service.**

### Build Time and Cost

- **Build time:** 4-6 weeks
- **Starting cost:** $100-250/mo

## Pricing Strategy

Managed service subscriptions. Price by traffic, contacts, seats, or support level.

## Customer Acquisition

1. Rank for privacy-first and GDPR-friendly alternatives.
2. Sell to agencies that need compliant tooling for clients.
3. Publish migration guides from Google Analytics and mainstream marketing suites.

## Unit Economics

- Start with low-touch onboarding if the price is below $50/mo.
- Use concierge onboarding if customers pay more than $100/mo.
- Watch support time per account; niche products can become service businesses if workflows are unclear.

## Common Pitfalls

1. Compliance claims must be precise and defensible.
2. Managed open source services need strong maintenance discipline.
3. Small customer counts mean support quality can make or break retention.

## Launch Checklist

### Pre-Launch
- [ ] Interview 10 target users and collect exact workflow language.
- [ ] Build the smallest workflow that produces a paid result.
- [ ] Add billing, onboarding, support email, and basic analytics.

### Launch
- [ ] Invite the first 20 prospects manually.
- [ ] Publish a comparison or teardown post for the existing manual process.
- [ ] Offer high-touch setup for the first paying customers.

### Post-Launch
- [ ] Turn repeated support questions into onboarding screens.
- [ ] Track activation, churn reasons, and top integrations.
- [ ] Add one acquisition channel at a time.
